I truly love side projects. Here are some interesting ones.

uguru.me

A growing Berkeley tutoring startup that I worked on in Fall 2013. As Lead Engineer, I developed the Flask backend for the site. Some tools I used include Heroku, SQLAlchemy, and PostgreSQL. I played a significant role in front-end development as well, working on responsive design and form validation. Uguru is now part of the 500 Startups accelerator.

Bump

[repo]

My hack for Camp Hackathona at Facebook during Summer 2013. The idea is simple: bump phones with another person and be thrown to their Facebook. We made this before Apple added NFC to their phones, so instead we use accelerometers to determine a “bump”, and then MongoDB’s geospatial queries to determine which two phones bumped. We built both an HTML5 app and a standalone iOS application.

Resistor Reader

[repo]

An Android application for working with resistor values. It’s simple, small, and user-friendly – and open source! This was my first mobile project, as well as my first Java project. I had zero experience with either going into it. The app is published to the Google Play Store.
